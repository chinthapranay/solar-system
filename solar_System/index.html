Learn about our Sun and each planet in the solar system

Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system, a nearly perfect sphere of hot plasma that provides energy for life on Earth.

Learn More

Mercury

The smallest planet in our solar system and closest to the Sun, Mercury is only slightly larger than Earth's Moon.

Unique Characteristics:

  • Closest planet to the Sun
  • Smallest planet in the solar system
  • Extreme temperature variations (-290°F to 800°F)
  • No atmosphere to retain heat
  • Surface covered in craters like the Moon

Venus

Similar in size to Earth, Venus spins slowly in the opposite direction from most planets with a thick atmosphere.

Unique Characteristics:

  • Hottest planet in the solar system (900°F)
  • Thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide
  • Rotates backwards (retrograde rotation)
  • Longest day of any planet (243 Earth days)
  • Surface pressure 92 times that of Earth

Earth

Our home planet is the only place we know of so far that's inhabited by living things with liquid water on the surface.

Unique Characteristics:

  • Only known planet with life
  • 71% of surface covered in water
  • Only planet with plate tectonics
  • Has a strong magnetic field
  • Only planet with oxygen in atmosphere

Mars

The fourth planet from the Sun, Mars is a dusty, cold, desert world with a very thin atmosphere.

Unique Characteristics:

  • Home to Olympus Mons, the tallest mountain in the solar system
  • Has the largest dust storms in the solar system
  • Two small moons (Phobos and Deimos)
  • Evidence of ancient water flows
  • Red color due to iron oxide (rust)

Jupiter

Jupiter is more than twice as massive than all other planets combined with a Great Red Spot storm.

Unique Characteristics:

  • Largest planet in the solar system
  • Fastest rotating planet (10-hour day)
  • Strongest winds in the solar system (400 mph)
  • 79 known moons
  • Great Red Spot storm larger than Earth

Saturn

Adorned with a dazzling system of icy rings, Saturn is unique among the planets.

Unique Characteristics:

  • Most extensive ring system in the solar system
  • 62 known moons
  • Least dense planet (could float in water)
  • Hexagonal storm at north pole
  • Rings made of ice and rock particles

Uranus

Uranus rotates at a nearly 90-degree angle from the plane of its orbit, making it unique among planets.

Unique Characteristics:

  • Rotates on its side (98° tilt)
  • 27 known moons
  • Blue-green color due to methane
  • Coldest planetary atmosphere (-371°F)
  • 13 faint rings

Neptune

Neptune is the windiest planet with the strongest winds in the solar system, reaching 1,500 miles per hour.

Unique Characteristics:

  • Coldest planet in the solar system
  • Strongest winds (1,500 mph)
  • 14 known moons
  • Great Dark Spot storm
  • Most distant planet from the Sun
