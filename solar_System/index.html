Explore the Sun and Planets

Learn about our Sun and each planet in the solar system

Sun

The star at the center of our solar system, a nearly perfect sphere of hot plasma providing energy for life on Earth.

Learn More

Mercury

The smallest planet and closest to the Sun, with extreme temperature variations and a cratered surface.

Unique Characteristics:

  • Closest planet to the Sun
  • Smallest planet
  • Extreme temperature variations
  • No significant atmosphere

Venus

Similar in size to Earth, Venus has a thick, toxic atmosphere and rotates backwards.

Unique Characteristics:

  • Hottest planet (surface temp ~900°F)
  • Thick CO2 atmosphere
  • Retrograde rotation
  • High surface pressure

Earth

Our home, the only known planet with liquid water on its surface and supporting life.

Unique Characteristics:

  • Supports life
  • Liquid water surface
  • Oxygen-rich atmosphere
  • Plate tectonics

Mars

The "Red Planet," a cold, dusty desert world with a thin atmosphere and evidence of past water.

Unique Characteristics:

  • Red color (iron oxide)
  • Olympus Mons (tallest volcano)
  • Valles Marineris (large canyon)
  • Thin atmosphere

Jupiter

The largest planet, a gas giant known for its Great Red Spot and numerous moons.

Unique Characteristics:

  • Largest planet
  • Great Red Spot (storm)
  • Fastest rotation
  • Many moons (e.g., Galilean moons)

Saturn

Famous for its extensive and complex ring system made of ice and rock particles.

Unique Characteristics:

  • Prominent ring system
  • Least dense planet
  • Many moons (e.g., Titan)
  • Hexagonal storm at North Pole

Uranus

An ice giant that rotates on its side, giving it extreme seasons.

Unique Characteristics:

  • Rotates on its side (~98° tilt)
  • Ice giant
  • Blue-green color (methane)
  • Faint ring system

Neptune

The most distant planet, an ice giant known for its strong winds and Great Dark Spot.

Unique Characteristics:

  • Most distant planet
  • Strongest winds
  • Ice giant
  • Great Dark Spot (storm feature)
